Self-Directed Learning (SDL) Resources

Extending learning beyond the session

Organized as 8 units — M1L1, M1L2, M2 through M7 — following the 5-tier, Bloom's-aligned progression: Getting Started, Building Up, Thinking Hard, Challenge Mode, Expert Level.

What this resource actually is

A rigorous, self-correcting alignment audit

Beyond the tiered learner activities, the SDL document's real contribution is an activity-by-activity audit against the AI4K12 Five Big Ideas framework — cross-checked directly against the dissertation's own Chapter 3, Table 3.5, with explicit ✓ Aligned, ⚠ Partial, and ✗ Not Aligned flags for every activity. Where the audit disagreed with the dissertation's own summary table, it says so — a methodological transparency worth highlighting rather than hiding.

Honest non-alignment

Not every activity has to be an AI concept

The document explicitly documents activities that serve pedagogical scaffolding rather than direct AI-concept delivery — and says so plainly, rather than force-fitting a Big Idea tag onto everything. Examples: the paper maze (Module 1) is a concretization scaffold for environment vocabulary, not an AI concept itself; the "Arrive at School on Time" planning task (Module 2) is a human-cognition transfer analogy with no AI agent involved; Module 5's product-availability discussion previews Module 6 rather than completing a demonstration in itself.

"Facilitators and self-directed learners alike benefit from knowing not only what to do in an activity, but why that activity was chosen and what it is meant to make visible." — from the source document's own design principle

Spotlight

Module 7: where all five Big Ideas converge

M7 is the only module in the curriculum where all five AI4K12 Big Ideas are substantively present, not peripheral: webcam gesture capture instantiates Perception; the trained model and Scratch game logic embody Representation & Reasoning; the complete collect-label-train-test-diagnose-retrain cycle is the curriculum's primary Learning module; IBM Watson's text commands and the gestural game interface constitute Natural Interaction; and diagnosing a model's failure due to insufficient training data is a direct, hands-on encounter with Societal Impact through data bias.
