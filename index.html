PhD Dissertation Research · University at Buffalo

A cognitive- and context-based approach to introducing foundational AI concepts to learners aged 10-14

AI literacy starts here — no coding required. Basics4AI empowers kids to understand, question, and shape AI, using games and everyday reasoning to make foundational AI concepts accessible before any technical implementation.

7 modules ~7.5 contact hours Ages 10–14 <$20/child kit cost IRB Protocol STUDY00009409
The core idea

Human reasoning first. Then problem context. Then AI abstraction.

AI literacy is no longer optional — the World Economic Forum projects that 85% of jobs in 2030 will require it. Yet most existing K-12 AI curricula lead with coding syntax and programming mechanics, overwhelming exactly the learners (ages 10-14) who are, developmentally, best positioned to reason about AI conceptually. Basics4AI inverts the usual order. Traditional AI education moves technology → algorithm → meaning. Basics4AI moves human reasoning → problem context → AI abstraction — introducing AI through learners' own observation, planning, and decision-making before any formal computational vocabulary appears.

The result is a seven-module, game- and activity-driven curriculum that requires no prior coding experience, no mathematics prerequisites, and no expensive technology — built to be developmentally appropriate, conceptually rigorous, and genuinely scalable.

From misconceptions to mastery

Media portrayals anthropomorphize AI. Current AI education is technology-centric. Developmentally appropriate resources for ages 10-14 are scarce. Basics4AI is designed to close all three gaps at once — making AI accessible, engaging, and genuinely understood.
