Chapter 5 · Research Methodology

A convergent mixed methods design

Quantitative and qualitative data are collected concurrently, with equal priority, and merged after independent analysis — not one explaining the other after the fact, but both illuminating the same phenomenon from different angles.

Why convergent, not sequential

Neither strand is subordinate to the other

A convergent design was chosen over an explanatory sequential design because the goal isn't to use qualitative findings to explain quantitative results after the fact — it's to determine whether standardized-instrument scores and participants' own words corroborate, complement, or diverge from one another. Both data types are collected during the same intervention period, analyzed independently, and then explicitly compared through joint displays.

"Do participant views from interviews, reflections, and standardized instruments converge or diverge?" — the question a convergent design is built to answer.

Sampling & power

~90 participants, purposefully and by convenience

Quantitative strand

Convenience sampling — all learners admitted to BuffaloPrep's programming who consent to participate, yielding a homogeneous sample sharing curriculum sequence and instructional conditions.

Qualitative strand

A purposively selected subsample of 4–10 participants, nested within the quantitative sample, chosen to represent a range of engagement profiles, prior knowledge, and demographic characteristics.

Statistical power: with a sample of 90, α = 0.05, and a conservative medium effect size (Cohen's f = 0.25), power analysis for a one-factor repeated-measures ANOVA across seven measurement occasions exceeds the conventional 0.80 threshold — the study is adequately powered to detect the anticipated effects across all four primary research questions.

Data collection

Concurrent, parallel, and independently rigorous

Quantitative surveys and qualitative reflections are administered concurrently at the close of each module (surveys first, to avoid priming effects). Post-program interviews follow only after all seven modules are complete, so participants can reflect on the full experience. Every research question is operationalized through parallel quantitative items and qualitative prompts targeting the same underlying construct — for example, RQ1's quantitative messaging-perception score is paralleled by an interview question asking whether content was easy or hard to follow.

Quantitative analysis

Repeated-measures ANOVA

Because the same participants are assessed across all seven modules, a one-factor repeated-measures model lets each participant serve as their own control — isolating change attributable to the curriculum rather than pre-existing differences between learners. Sphericity is checked via Mauchly's test, with Greenhouse-Geisser correction applied where violated; effect sizes are reported as partial eta-squared. Three safeguards address the standard threats to repeated-measures validity: a ~5-week pre/post interval (guards against practice effects), module-specific item wording (reduces cross-module contamination), and the qualitative strand itself as an independent check — if a quantitative dip reflects fatigue rather than genuine response, it should surface in participants' own words.

Qualitative analysis

Braun & Clarke's six-phase thematic analysis

Written reflections and interview transcripts are analyzed inductively — familiarization, initial coding, searching for themes, reviewing and refining, defining and naming, and producing the final report — without imposing a priori conceptual categories.

A methodological limitation, stated plainly

As a single-researcher dissertation project, formal intercoder reliability with a human coding team isn't feasible. The researcher plans exploratory, supplementary use of LLM-assisted thematic analysis (following De Paoli, 2024) as an additional point of comparison and reflexivity — explicitly not as an official coder, and never substituting for the researcher's own interpretive judgment.

Integration

Joint displays and meta-inference

After independent analysis, the two datasets are merged and compared through joint-display tables organized around shared constructs — AI conceptual understanding, misconceptions, messaging perception, cognitive engagement, motivation. The goal is not merely to show agreement: divergence between the two strands is examined just as carefully, since it can reveal genuine complexity in learners' experience that a Likert scale alone can't capture. This produces meta-inferences — conclusions that emerge only from combining both strands, not available from either one independently.

Data pipeline

Built to avoid the usual research-data headaches

Early pre-pilot data collection via Qualtrics required extensive manual CSV cleaning, duplicate adjudication, and external scoring scripts before analysis could begin — and risked losing the "in-the-moment" quality of responses if survey completion was delayed. In response, the Basics4AI platform's own student dashboard was built to embed validation, automated scoring, and analysis-ready dataset construction directly into data collection: a single responses.db source of truth, YAML-defined instrument/scoring ontologies, and a module-locked flow that prevents advancing without completing that module's instruments — producing clean, wide-format data ready for repeated-measures analysis without manual merging.
