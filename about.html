About the researcher & developer

Soumya K. De

AI Literacy Researcher, University at Buffalo — Dept. of Learning & Instruction. Translating AI systems into cognitively grounded learning, with 20+ years building and deploying real-world technical systems before turning to education research.

Basics4AI is designed and built by a researcher who spent two decades as a systems architect and entrepreneur before turning to education research — not the usual path into AI-literacy curriculum design, and precisely what shaped the platform's approach.

Soumya spent over 20 years across Silicon Valley and India in wireless systems engineering, technical marketing, and enterprise-scale systems integration — including roles at Cisco Systems and Symbol Technologies (now Motorola), followed by 17 years founding and leading two technology companies delivering IP/IoT automation, industrial monitoring, and energy systems for clients including Tata Motors, Tata Steel, Coal India Ltd., and the National Council of Science Museums (Government of India). That work meant taking a concept all the way from grant proposal to a deployed, field-tested system used by real operators under real constraints — not a lab demo.

Since 2022, that same execution discipline has been applied to Basics4AI: a cognitive- and context-based AI literacy curriculum for learners aged 10-14. As a PhD candidate in the University at Buffalo's Department of Learning & Instruction, Soumya designed, piloted, and evaluated the curriculum across libraries, community centers, and youth programs in Western New York — and, when no existing platform could support the mixed-methods data collection the research required, built one (the analytics platform behind Basics4AI is presented separately at Get2KnoUrLearners).

Basics4AI is currently being piloted under IRB Protocol STUDY00009409 with approximately 90 students at BuffaloPrep and the Town of Amherst Youth and Recreation Department.

Why this background

The skills behind the curriculum

Basics4AI required conceptualizing a cognitive-science-grounded curriculum, grounding it in real learning-sciences theory, and then actually piloting and evaluating it in real community settings — not just one of those things.

Systems & instructional architecture

Two decades designing and deploying multi-layer technical systems translated directly into the curriculum's own layered design — cognitive foundations, instructional design frameworks (ADDIE/UCIT), pedagogical operationalization (PBL/GBL), and facilitator support infrastructure.

Applied statistics & assessment design

An Advanced Certificate in Applied Statistical Analysis underlies the study's convergent mixed-methods evaluation framework — validated instruments, repeated-measures ANOVA, and the same psychometric rigor a peer-reviewed study requires.

AI evaluation & hands-on implementation

Python-LLM integration and rapid prototyping in Streamlit aren't outsourced skills — the LLM-assisted thematic analysis approach used in this research was built and applied directly.

Learning sciences & curriculum design

PhD coursework in Learning & Instruction grounds the curriculum's theoretical foundations — Piaget, Vygotsky, conceptual change theory, self-determination theory — cited throughout this site, not applied after the fact.

Program execution at scale

Seventeen years running technology companies means the curriculum was designed to actually reach real classrooms — piloted with real students, real facilitators, and a real professional-development pathway, not left as an academic exercise.

Translating complexity for non-specialists

Years in technical marketing and pre-sales engineering — making complex systems understandable to non-technical audiences — are exactly why Basics4AI can be delivered by volunteer facilitators with no AI or coding background.
