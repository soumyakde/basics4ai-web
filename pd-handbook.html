About
Volunteer Facilitator Professional Development Program

Training a community of facilitators, not just delivering a curriculum

A structured, evidence-based system to prepare community volunteers — with no prior AI or computer-science teaching experience — to deliver Basics4AI across libraries, community centers, and after-school programs.

4 phases6–8 weeks per cohort10 program documents
Theoretical grounding

Seven converging frameworks

Desimone's 5 features of effective PD, TPACK's three knowledge domains, Knowles' andragogy (adult learning principles), the Gradual Release of Responsibility model, Wenger's Communities of Practice, Universal Design for Learning, and explicit AI4K12/CSTA standards alignment — chosen because they directly address the challenge of preparing non-specialist volunteers to facilitate inquiry-based AI literacy instruction for diverse youth audiences.

Program architecture

The 4-phase model

Phase 1 · ~8 hrs

LEARN IT — Asynchronous Self-Study

Facilitators experience all 7 modules as a student first — completing the student workbook, not the facilitator guide — and identify their own knowledge gaps before ever teaching.

Phase 2 · 16 hrs

TEACH IT — 2-Day Intensive Workshop

Day 1: content + pedagogy, experiencing every module in compressed form. Day 2: facilitation practice — microteaching, peer feedback, logistics, and role-played difficult scenarios.

Phase 3 · 2–3 weeks

DO IT — Supported Practice

2–4 real deliveries, co-facilitated with a certified trainer or experienced facilitator, each followed by a structured observation form and a 30-minute coaching debrief.

Phase 4 · Ongoing

SUSTAIN IT — Community of Practice

Monthly virtual check-ins, a shared facilitation log, peer lesson study, and an annual in-person refresh to keep facilitation quality current as the curriculum evolves.

Content knowledge scope

What facilitators need to know

AI Concepts

Agents, sensors/actuators, supervised vs. unsupervised vs. reinforcement learning, hallucination, bias.

Algorithms & Problem Solving

Means-end analysis, decision trees, game trees, Minimax, DFS, constraint satisfaction.

Probability & Uncertainty

Deterministic vs. stochastic environments, compound probability, irreducible uncertainty.

Natural Language Processing

Inferencing, Script Theory, intent recognition, Winograd Schema, WinoBias.

Machine Learning Engineering

The full supervised learning cycle — collect, label, train, test, improve — and why data variety matters.

AI Ethics & Responsibility

The 4-question AI checklist, critical AI consumption, shared responsibility, data responsibility.

Child safety

A precondition of certification, not an afterthought

Because every session involves direct, recurring contact with children ages 10-14 in community settings, no volunteer facilitates independently — or is left alone with students — until a cleared background check is on file, the two-adult rule is in place, a signed code of conduct is on record, and mandated-reporter orientation is complete. Where a host venue has its own, stricter policy, that policy governs.
